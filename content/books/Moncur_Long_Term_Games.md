<div class="book-container">
    <div class="book-header">
        <h1 class="book-title">🔁 Play Long-Term Games with Long-Term People</h1>
        <p class="book-subtitle">By Kenneth Moncur</p>
    </div>

    <div class="intro-section">
        <p>Let me tell you something that's sacred if you want to build real wealth, real impact, and real legacy — especially in a place like The Bahamas:</p>
        <blockquote>Play long-term games with long-term people.</blockquote>
        <p>In this country, people like to move fast. They like quick licks, side hustles, and what they can get <em>right now</em>. But if you're trying to build something that outlives you, that mindset will sink you.</p>
    </div>

    <div class="games-section">
        <h3>🧠 What Does It Mean?</h3>
        <p>A <strong>long-term game</strong> is any vision, business, or mission that compounds over time:</p>
        <ul>
            <li>A platform that grows stronger with every user</li>
            <li>A reputation that builds over 10+ years</li>
            <li>A team that learns how to move as one unit</li>
        </ul>
        
        <p><strong>Long-term people</strong> are those who:</p>
        <ul>
            <li>Don't switch up when things get hard</li>
            <li>See what you're building — even when it's small</li>
            <li>Aren't in it for clout — they're in it for impact</li>
        </ul>
    </div>

    <div class="games-section">
        <h3>🇧🇸 Why It Matters in The Bahamas</h3>
        <p>Because here:</p>
        <ul>
            <li>Everyone knows everyone</li>
            <li>Word travels fast</li>
            <li>The culture leans toward <strong>"look good now, suffer later"</strong></li>
        </ul>
        
        <p>But the real ones? They build <strong>quiet</strong>, smart, and deep.</p>
        
        <p>You need:</p>
        <ul>
            <li>Partners who don't sell out at the first contract</li>
            <li>Friends who challenge your vision but stay loyal</li>
            <li>Teammates who aren't chasing the next political wave or money run</li>
        </ul>
        
        <blockquote>If your crew's loyalty expires in election season — you need a new crew.</blockquote>
    </div>

    <div class="games-section">
        <h3>🔍 How to Spot Long-Term People</h3>
        <p>Look for those who:</p>
        <ul>
            <li>Say "we" more than "me"</li>
            <li>Document, refine, and revisit their craft</li>
            <li>Don't gossip — they build</li>
            <li>Are already doing the work before the camera shows up</li>
        </ul>
    </div>

    <div class="games-section">
        <h3>🔁 What Long-Term Games Look Like in The Bahamas</h3>
        <ul>
            <li>Building a national education platform (Kemis Academy)</li>
            <li>Creating a local tech stack that outlasts government turnover (VerityOS)</li>
            <li>Launching a sovereign finance system with goal-based saving (Minty)</li>
            <li>Building the next generation of thinkers, creators, and digital citizens</li>
        </ul>
        
        <p>These aren't side hustles — they're <strong>nation-building projects</strong> disguised as apps.</p>
    </div>

    <div class="games-section">
        <h3>⚠️ What to Avoid</h3>
        <ul>
            <li>Short-term hype men</li>
            <li>People who disappear after one setback</li>
            <li>"All talk, no build" energy</li>
            <li>Folks chasing grants, not outcomes</li>
        </ul>
    </div>

    <div class="final-word">
        <h3>✅ Final Word</h3>
        <p><strong>If you want to go far, go together — but only with people who plan to finish the race.</strong><br>
        Play long games with people who think legacy, not likes.</p>
        <p>In a place like The Bahamas, that's how you outlast the chaos and build systems that actually serve the people.</p>
    </div>

    <div class="author-signature">
        –– Kenneth Moncur
    </div>
</div>
