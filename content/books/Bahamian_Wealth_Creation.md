<div class="book-container">
    <div class="book-header">
        <h1 class="book-title">💰 How Wealth Is Created: The Bahamian Perspective</h1>
        <p class="book-subtitle">A blueprint for understanding the flow of value, ownership, and opportunity across our islands and beyond.</p>
    </div>

    <div class="wealth-principle">
        <h3>🧱 1. Wealth is Not Just Money — It's Value Ownership</h3>
        <p><strong>Wealth</strong> is created when you <strong>control something valuable</strong> — and <strong>others pay to access it</strong>.</p>
        
        <p>In The Bahamas, most people only interact with money as:</p>
        <ul>
            <li>Workers (trading time for dollars)</li>
            <li>Consumers (spending what little they earn)</li>
        </ul>
        
        <p>But <strong>real wealth comes from ownership</strong>:</p>
        <ul>
            <li>Land</li>
            <li>Businesses</li>
            <li>Natural resources</li>
            <li>Software or IP</li>
            <li>Distribution networks (like email lists or TikTok audiences)</li>
        </ul>
        
        <blockquote>If you don't own it — you're renting your life.</blockquote>
    </div>

    <div class="wealth-principle">
        <h3>🌴 2. Tourism Makes Money — But It Doesn't Build Wealth</h3>
        <p>The Bahamas' #1 industry is tourism. But here's the hard truth:</p>
        <ul>
            <li>Most resorts are <strong>foreign-owned</strong></li>
            <li>Profits are <strong>exported</strong></li>
            <li>Local jobs are mostly <strong>low-wage and temporary</strong></li>
        </ul>
        
        <p>So while it <em>brings in revenue</em>, it doesn't create <strong>Bahamian wealth</strong> — it just <strong>rents out paradise</strong>.</p>
        
        <p>True wealth means <strong>Bahamians owning</strong>:</p>
        <ul>
            <li>Boutique hotels</li>
            <li>Transportation and tours</li>
            <li>Digital platforms that attract visitors</li>
            <li>Products and services tourists <em>buy and take home</em></li>
        </ul>
    </div>

    <div class="wealth-principle">
        <h3>🌾 3. Natural Resources Are Trapped Wealth</h3>
        <p>The Bahamas has <strong>untapped billions</strong> in:</p>
        <ul>
            <li>Aragonite</li>
            <li>Fisheries</li>
            <li>Sand</li>
            <li>Salt</li>
            <li>Rare minerals</li>
            <li>Strategic marine territory</li>
        </ul>
        
        <p>Yet:</p>
        <ul>
            <li>Most of it is <strong>leased or exploited without public benefit</strong></li>
            <li>The people don't see returns from <strong>what's under their own feet</strong></li>
        </ul>
        
        <blockquote>Wealth is not just what you have — it's what you <strong>control and monetize.</strong></blockquote>
    </div>

    <div class="wealth-principle">
        <h3>💡 4. Digital Platforms = Exportable Wealth</h3>
        <p>Instead of just exporting lobster and sand, <strong>export ideas</strong>:</p>
        <ul>
            <li>Online courses (Kemis Academy model)</li>
            <li>Software (VerityOS-style platforms)</li>
            <li>Bahamian music, art, and stories as global media</li>
            <li>NFTs or tokens backed by local culture and assets</li>
        </ul>
        
        <p>The internet removes the barrier of location — now a teen in Andros can build apps that generate USD daily.</p>
    </div>

    <div class="wealth-principle">
        <h3>🏦 5. Financial Systems Must Be Understood, Then Hacked</h3>
        <p>Most Bahamians don't learn about:</p>
        <ul>
            <li>Compound interest</li>
            <li>Business credit</li>
            <li>Sovereign wealth funds</li>
            <li>Investment funds</li>
            <li>Real estate equity</li>
        </ul>
        
        <p>So they stay dependent on:</p>
        <ul>
            <li>Payroll</li>
            <li>Government jobs</li>
            <li>Short-term side hustles</li>
        </ul>
        
        <p>Real wealth builders:</p>
        <ul>
            <li>Use <strong>capital as a tool</strong></li>
            <li>Learn <strong>how money moves</strong></li>
            <li>Build systems to <strong>own the flow of cash, not chase the crumbs</strong></li>
        </ul>
    </div>

    <div class="wealth-principle">
        <h3>📈 6. Wealth Is Created by Systems, Not Hustles</h3>
        <p>The "grind" culture only gets you so far.</p>
        
        <blockquote>A hustle ends when you stop working. A system pays you while you sleep.</blockquote>
        
        <p>Systems that create Bahamian wealth:</p>
        <ul>
            <li>A business with digital products + automation</li>
            <li>A co-op that exports local crops with profit-sharing</li>
            <li>An online store that sells cultural merch worldwide</li>
            <li>A local app that processes 10,000 payments a month</li>
        </ul>
    </div>

    <div class="wealth-principle">
        <h3>🧠 7. Wealth Without Wisdom = Collapse</h3>
        <p>In The Bahamas, we've seen:</p>
        <ul>
            <li>Lottery winners go broke</li>
            <li>Land inherited, then sold cheap</li>
            <li>Fast money burned by poor planning</li>
        </ul>
        
        <p>Wealth must be <strong>paired with knowledge</strong>:</p>
        <ul>
            <li>Legal literacy</li>
            <li>Tax planning</li>
            <li>Digital security</li>
            <li>Legacy design (trusts, wills, IP ownership)</li>
        </ul>
    </div>

        <p>The <strong>goal is to move up the pyramid</strong> — not stay stuck at the bottom.</p>
    </div>

    <div class="blocks-section">
        <h3>🚨 What Blocks Bahamian Wealth Creation?</h3>
        <ul>
            <li>Lack of access to startup capital</li>
            <li>Gatekeeping in banks, media, and political circles</li>
            <li>Imported mindsets of dependency</li>
            <li>Fear of systems we don't understand (crypto, tech, legal structures)</li>
        </ul>
    </div>

    <div class="unlocks-section">
        <h3>✅ What Unlocks It?</h3>
        <ul>
            <li>Ownership over what we build, share, and sell</li>
            <li>Systems that scale: software, exports, royalties, digital assets</li>
            <li>Cultural confidence — knowing our stories and skills have global value</li>
            <li>Education that's <strong>sovereign, strategic, and unapologetic</strong></li>
        </ul>
    </div>
